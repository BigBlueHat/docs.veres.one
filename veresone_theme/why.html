Autonomous

Part of the Veres One mission is to provide an identity infrastructure that maximized user autonomy.

Some call this autonomy Self sovereign identity, which means that any person or organization in the world can create and control their identifiers.

This puts users at the center of their identity management, enabling them to aggregate data about themselves rather than various organizations outside their control. User-control also makes is possible for entities to migrate their data to a location of their choice.

Privacy Aware

Veres One seeks to maximize user privacy.

The user-centric architecture means that a user can reduce the amount of personal data shared because they can selective choose which credentials, or even parts of credentials, are shared with whom.

Multiple identifiers enable users to create and compartmentalize different profiles, such as personal, social, and professional profiles, in a way that cannot be correlated.

The fact that Veres One is a tokenless network makes it more difficult to track the exchange of identity information. However, the way your agent or digital wallet provider manages your information plays a significant role in how your privacy is preserved and how easily correlatable your activities are.

Open

Veres One is based entirely on international web standards, open specifications, and has an ethical open source license.

There Veres One Project will always strive to be 100% compliant with the standards it uses and will help create new standards where none exist via the W3C and other pertinent standards organizations.

Broad implementation and interoperability can only be achieved if all aspects of the system are documented and standardized in a patent-free and royalty-free manner while ensuring that the network is properly funded.
